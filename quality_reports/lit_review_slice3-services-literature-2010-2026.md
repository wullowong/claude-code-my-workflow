## Summary

The Chinese productive ag-services literature is large and methodologically mature. Three clean facts emerge. **First**, services → labor reallocation in China is an established, replicated finding at the household level: Zou & Mishra (2024, *AEPP*), Chen (2025, *RoDE*), Pan, Gao & Ji (2025, *RoDE*), and a substantial Chinese-language complement (Yang & Cai 2020, *J. Ag. Modernization*; Wu, Bai & Zhang 2025, same journal) have together built a robust base for the second leg of the project's chain. **Second**, the *services literature itself* has begun absorbing insurance as a small side variable, and at least one Chinese-language paper — **Ling Tao, Lan Haoge, Cui Weichuan & Zhao Guiqin (2025, *Journal of Finance and Economics* / 财经研究) "From Risk Protection to Production Empowerment: Can Agricultural Insurance Promote Farmers' Adoption of Productive Services?"** — directly estimates the **first leg** of the project's chain (insurance → productive-service adoption) on a 748-household, 10-province CRRS survey, with positive and significant effects. The mediator is **planted-area expansion**, not labor reallocation. **Third**, two Chinese-language papers test the boundary in the opposite direction from the project: **Chen & Gu (2026, *CAER*)** finds insurance increases the *agricultural* labor share via land-rental expansion (not via services), and **Chai, Ren & Li (2025, *J. Agrotechnical Economics*, forthcoming)** finds the same direction via agricultural-income-expectation and tech-investment mediators (also not via services). This is a non-trivial directional threat to the project's hypothesis, beyond any framing-overlap concern.

The wedge survives but with a *narrowed* contribution claim. No paper located — Chinese- or English-language — estimates the *full* chain insurance → productive services (托管 / 社会化服务) → household labor reallocation as a mediated chain at the household level. The two halves are now both individually estimated in the Chinese literature (Ling Tao et al. 2025 for the first leg; Yang & Cai 2020 / Wu, Bai & Zhang 2025 / Zou & Mishra 2024 for the second). The project's contribution becomes: *first paper to bolt the two legs together as a single mediated chain in a CFPS household panel design with explicit policy-bundle controls, and to honestly confront the directional puzzle that two Chinese-language papers find the opposite labor-allocation direction*. This is a more defensible — and arguably more interesting — framing than the original "first paper to estimate services-mediated insurance → labor."

## Wedge stress-test (Chinese-language angle)

**The single most important question for this slice: does any Chinese-language paper estimate insurance → productive services → labor at the household level?**

**Answer (web-only first pass, ~85% confidence): NO. The full mediated chain has not been estimated in any paper located.** But the boundary is *much* tighter than Slice 2 estimated. Three Chinese-language papers individually preempt pieces of the chain or operate adjacent to it:

1. **Ling Tao, Lan Haoge, Cui Weichuan & Zhao Guiqin (2025), *Journal of Finance and Economics* (财经研究) 51(10): 125–139** — "From Risk Protection to Production Empowerment: Can Agricultural Insurance Promote Farmers' Adoption of Productive Services?" Uses 2020 China Rural Revitalization Survey (CRRS), 748 households across 10 provinces. Tests insurance (binary participation + premium expenditure) → productive-service expenditure across 5 production stages (land prep, sowing, pesticide, fertilization, harvest+transport). Finds significant positive effects, stronger in technology-intensive than labor-intensive stages. **Headline: insurance roughly doubles productive-service spending among adopting households.** Operative mechanism for service adoption is planted-area expansion. **Important post-CoVe correction:** the paper *does* additionally test labor (off-farm employment 外出务工) as a mechanism candidate, finding insurance → off-farm employment is significant and positive, but off-farm work → service adoption is null (so labor is not the operative channel for services). This means the upstream "insurance → labor (off-farm)" leg is partially preempted at the household level — the project's full mediated chain (insurance → *services* → labor) is still untested, but a side mechanism in Ling Tao et al. already establishes that insurance shifts labor off-farm in CRRS data. *(Bibliographic + substantive verified by CoVe 2026-05-08 via SUFE journal portal.)*

2. **Chen, Liuyan & Gu, Haiying (2026), *China Agricultural Economic Review* 18(2)** — "Impact of agricultural insurance on labor allocation." Uses 2019–2021 National Fixed-Point Survey panel (RCRE / 农村固定观察点) for wheat, maize, rice. Tests insurance → household agricultural labor share. **Direction: insurance INCREASES the household agricultural labor share (opposite of the project's hypothesis).** Mediator: land transfer (insurance facilitates land rentals for small-scale farmers under 10 mu, which increases on-farm labor). Services / 社会化服务 / 托管 are **not** the tested mediator. Published in Chinese-flagship English-medium journal CAER; uses Chinese micro-panel data.

3. **Chai Zhihui, Ren Jing & Li Sainan (2025)**, "农业保险对农户劳动力资源配置的影响" (Effect of agricultural insurance on household labor resource allocation), forthcoming in 农业技术经济 (*Journal of Agrotechnical Economics*), online first 7 Jan 2025, DOI 10.13246/j.cnki.jae.20250107.001. **CoVe 2026-05-08: corrected from initially-flagged "Sun, Mi & Sang" attribution — actual authors are at Inner Mongolia Agricultural University (Chai, Ren) and Inner Mongolia Academy of Social Sciences (Li); the CARD-ZJU page was a re-posting/announcement, not the paper's home venue.** 4-province grain-household survey, 984 households, Oprobit/Bioprobit/CMP models. **Direction: insurance promotes labor INTO agricultural sector** (weakens 离农 tendency); consistent with Chen & Gu (2026). Mediators (corrected): **agricultural-income expectation + agricultural-technology investment** — NOT land-rental (which is Chen & Gu's mediator). So this is a *distinct* threat with a different mechanism, not a duplicate of Chen & Gu. Services / 托管 not tested as mediator. Heterogeneity: stronger effects on larger-area, age <45, lower-education households.

**Adjacent papers that do NOT estimate the full chain but tighten the boundary:**

- **Fu, Qin & Wang (2022), 资源科学 / *Resources Science* 44(10): 1980–1993** — "Effect of agricultural insurance on production factor allocation and its mechanism." Tests insurance → factor allocation (land + labor + capital) at the *macro* level via "facilitating modern agriculture development" framing. [UNVERIFIED: whether labor outcome is tested at household or province level — paper page returned only metadata.] Likely province-level analysis given outlet.
- **Yan, Yi & Zhang (2024), 中国农村经济 / *Chinese Rural Economy* (Slice 1 anchor)** — fertilizer reduction effect of insurance via credit-and-information dual constraints; not a labor or services paper, but the same Yi Fujin / Nanjing Ag U group is most likely to be the first to publish the full chain — flagged for Slice 3 monitoring.
- **The CCAP-PKU working paper (2019)** "参加农业保险能优化要素配置吗？" (Can participating in agricultural insurance optimize factor allocation?) — tests insurance → production-factor allocation. [UNVERIFIED: whether services or labor are the outcomes; PDF binary content not extractable.]

## Cluster (c): ⚠⚠ Insurance × services × labor (Chinese-language)

**Critical finding: 0 papers estimate the full chain insurance → services (托管 / 农业社会化服务) → household labor reallocation as a single mediated design. But two halves of the chain are individually estimated in the Chinese literature, and the boundary is much tighter than Slice 2 anticipated.**

### ⚠ THE TIGHTEST PREEMPTING PAPER — Ling, Tao; Lan, Haoge; Cui, Weichuan & Zhao, Guiqin (2025), *Journal of Finance and Economics* (财经研究) 51(10): 125–139
"从风险保障到生产赋能：农业保险能否促进农户采用生产性服务？" (From Risk Protection to Production Empowerment: Can Agricultural Insurance Promote Farmers' Adoption of Productive Services?). **The first Chinese-language paper to directly estimate the first leg of the project's chain at the household level.** All four authors are at Shanghai University of Finance and Economics, School of Finance; Zhao Guiqin is corresponding author. CRRS 2020 (China Rural Revitalization Survey), 748 households across 10 provinces. Treatment: insurance participation + premium expenditure. Outcome: productive-service expenditure across 5 production stages (land prep, sowing, pesticide, fertilization, harvest+transport), categorized as labor- vs. technology-intensive. **Headline: insurance roughly doubles productive-service spending among adopting households (~+103.7%).** Effects stronger in technology-intensive than labor-intensive segments. **Operative mechanism for service adoption is planted-area expansion. CoVe correction: the paper DOES additionally test labor (off-farm employment 外出务工) as a mechanism candidate — insurance → off-farm employment is significant and positive, but off-farm work → service adoption is null.** So labor is examined as a side mechanism (with the project-supportive direction) but is not the operative channel for service adoption itself. Disaster risk and land fragmentation positively moderate the effects. *(Bibliographic + substantive verified by CoVe 2026-05-08 via SUFE journal portal HTML.)* **Implications for project framing:** the narrow "first to estimate the full chain" claim survives but two broader claims do NOT: (i) "first paper on insurance → services in China" — Ling Tao et al. own this; (ii) "first paper on insurance → off-farm labor in China at the household level via CRRS" — Ling Tao et al.'s mechanism finding establishes this in the project's preferred direction. Project must cite Ling Tao et al. (2025) explicitly as the upstream-leg incumbent on BOTH service adoption and (as a mechanism) off-farm labor, and position the contribution as *the first paper to estimate the full mediated chain (insurance → services → labor) with services as the mediator at the household level on a panel design*.

### ⚠ DIRECTIONAL THREAT — Chen, Liuyan & Gu, Haiying (2026), *China Agricultural Economic Review* 18(2), DOI 10.1108/CAER-12-2024-0460
"Impact of agricultural insurance on labor allocation." 2019–2021 National Fixed-Point Survey (RCRE) panel for wheat, maize, rice. Insurance → household agricultural labor share, **POSITIVE direction (labor INTO agriculture).** Mediator: land transfer (insurance increases probability and area of land rentals for small-scale farmers under 10 mu). Services / 托管 / 社会化服务 are **not** the tested mediator. **Implications for project framing:** the project's hypothesized direction (insurance → labor OUT of own-farm cultivation) is empirically *opposed* by this paper. The project must explicitly address why CFPS-household + province-year-PAY intensity + service-mediated chain would predict the opposite direction from a land-transfer-mediated chain on RCRE data. The cleanest defensible interpretation: services and land-rental are *competing* margins of farm reorganization, with services-mediated households reallocating labor *off-farm* and land-rental households consolidating *more* on-farm labor (the renter expands cultivated area). The project's heterogeneity-by-farm-size cut becomes load-bearing under this interpretation.

## Synthesis: Slice 1 + 2 + 3 wedge verdict

**Combined-slice headline.** The project's distinctive contribution claim — "first paper to estimate insurance → services → labor reallocation as a mediated chain at the household level in China" — survives all three slices at ~85% confidence (Slice 3 web-only, conditional on CoVe completing CNKI-targeted stress-test). **But it survives in a *narrowed* form** relative to the original spec.

**The wedge in three precise propositions.**
1. **No paper** in any language estimates the full mediated chain insurance → services (托管 / 农业社会化服务) → household labor reallocation as a single design.
2. The first leg (insurance → services) has been estimated in Chinese-language work — Ling Tao et al. (2025, 财经研究) — on a CRRS sample with the planted-area-expansion mediator and *no labor outcome*.
3. The second leg (services → labor reallocation) has been estimated in English-language work (Zou & Mishra 2024, *AEPP*; Chen 2025, *RoDE*; Pan, Gao & Ji 2025, *RoDE*) and Chinese-language work (Yang & Cai 2020, *J. Ag. Modernization*; Wu, Bai & Zhang 2025) on CFPS and survey data, with no insurance treatment.

**The directional puzzle the project must now address.** Two papers find insurance increases the *agricultural* labor share at the household level, with **two different mediators** — both of which are *opposite* in direction to the project's hypothesis:

- **Chen & Gu (2026, *CAER*)** — mediator is **land-rental expansion** (insurance facilitates land rentals for small-scale farmers under 10 mu).
- **Chai, Ren & Li (2025, *J. Agrotechnical Economics*, forthcoming)** — mediators are **agricultural-income expectations + agricultural-technology investment** (NOT land-rental; CoVe correction from initial Slice 3 draft, which mis-attributed the paper to "Sun, Mi & Sang" and conflated its channels with Chen & Gu's).

A third paper, **Ling Tao et al. (2025, *财经研究*)**, runs a side mechanism check finding insurance → off-farm employment is significant and positive — *consistent* with the project's hypothesized direction. So the directional question in the Chinese-language literature is genuinely contested.

The project's defensible interpretation: there are at least three distinct margins of farm reorganization triggered by insurance, all running through different mediators:
1. Services / 托管 → off-farm labor reallocation (project's hypothesis; supported by Chen 2025 RoDE on services → labor without insurance, and by Ling Tao 2025's side mechanism on insurance → off-farm).
2. Land-rental → on-farm labor consolidation (Chen & Gu 2026 finding for small-scale renters-in).
3. Income-expectation + tech-investment → on-farm labor consolidation (Chai, Ren & Li 2025 finding for grain producers).

**The heterogeneity-by-farm-size cut now becomes load-bearing rather than supplementary** — small-and-fragmented households mediated by services should reallocate off-farm; mid-size renter-in households or grain-producing households mediated by land-transfer or tech-investment should consolidate on-farm. The project's CFPS-panel + province-year-PAY-intensity design must be calibrated to detect all three margins, with explicit decomposition.

**Defensible contribution claim post-Slice 3:**

> "First household-panel paper to estimate an insurance → productive-services → labor-reallocation mediated chain in Chinese smallholder agriculture, distinguishing service-mediated from (i) land-rental-mediated (Chen & Gu 2026), (ii) income-expectation + tech-investment-mediated (Chai, Ren & Li 2025), and (iii) technology-mediated (Wang et al. 2025) chains. Resolves the directional discrepancy with the Chinese-language literature by separating heterogeneity-by-farm-size margins. Cites Ling Tao et al.
